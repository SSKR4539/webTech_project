Auditya Venkatesh normally called as ADUI been a working photographer for over 10 years, and got a chance to work on a wide range of assignments. From working with one of India’s largest volunteer organisations to document their work across India, shooting with a leading sports brand with the world’s fastest man, creating content with the world’s leading smartphone manufacturer or shooting international travel shows across the world, He was always worked hard to create aesthetic visual imagery with compelling stories.

Audi in an interview:
One of them is Auditya Venkatesh, a CA student who switched his career path to follow his passion for photography.

I strongly believe that your upbringing has a large part to play in the whatever you create. My family always travelled together, finding the smallest of excuses to go discover a new place, live a new experience, and share our stories with the people we meet, so when I became a photographer, that’s what I started doing subconsciously.

Through my travels I have had the opportunity of working on a wide range of assignments. From shadowing one of India’s largest volunteer organisations to document their work across India, Documenting the aftermath of life following the tragic earthquake that stuck Nepal, working with the world’s fastest man and one of the world’s largest sports brands, creating content with the world’s leading smartphone manufacturers, capturing the ambience of leading hospitality chains in the country, or shooting international travel shows across the world, I’ve always worked hard to create aesthetic visual imagery with compelling stories. My work has been featured across the world through exhibitions, features and publications, online and offline.

On a more personal note, I love driving. I’ve driven all over the country on my personal project Stay Routed. I love dogs. I love sharing my journey and experience. I’ve spoken internationally through various forums. I’ve spoke multiple times at TEDx, the Adobe Make It Summit in Sydney, the Under 25 Summit, many other leading institutions and corporations across India, . I love and believe in the power of social media, and built a community called Audi Photography. Over time we have managed to build a community of over a million people. You can join us through the links below.

How has Facebook helped you in growing?

Just networking I think, I mean that concept has helped me a lot. First big brands have got in touch with me through Facebook. A lot of times when I was in a random city and there was no body I knew and I put up a status and someone helped me out. Now I’m on Instagram and Snapchat as well.

His photography page on Facebook, started in 2010, is titled Audi Photography and has got nearly 6,50,000 followers as of now. He was featured among the 16 people to look out for in 2016 by The Indian Express, alongside Tamil superstar Surya and acclaimed Bollywood director Ashutosh Gowariker.
